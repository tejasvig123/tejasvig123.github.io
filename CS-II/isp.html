Explore My ISP Journey

Here you will learn all about the ISP I did last year, including the ups and downs, the concept, and the conclusion.

The concept of the game is based on an old Mario game called Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door. The game features 10 levels, each with an increasing number of doors to choose from. The player must guess which door leads to the next level. You play as "Zowser," Bowser's alternate double, trying to save "Meek" Meach from the evil Zario.

The motivation for this game came from my and my partner's love for retro Mario games. We also wanted to avoid making a platformer due to time constraints. Some features included enqueueTransitionScene, allowing doors to open when pressing the correct key.

Challenges included scrapping a character with running, stopping, and jumping animations due to time constraints. We also eliminated a final boss battle between Zowser and Zario. However, the custom artwork and increasing door count per level remained as planned.

Through this project, I learned a lot about Boolean Algebra, conditionals, loops, game design, and user interfaces. I also gained experience reading binary, hexadecimal, and other coding concepts.
